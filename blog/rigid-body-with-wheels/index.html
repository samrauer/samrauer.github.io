Dynamics of a spacecraft with reaction wheels

Notation guide

  • FA\rmF_\rmAFA​: inertial frame
  • FB\rmF_\rmBFB​: body fixed frame
  • FS\rmF_\rmSFS​: reaction wheel fixed frame
  • (ı^S,ȷ^S,k^S)(\ihat_\rmS,\jhat_\rmS,\khat_\rmS)(^S​,^​S​,k^S​): the basis vectors of FS\rmF_\rmSFS​
  • ω⇀B/A\vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA}ω⇀B/A​: angular velocity of FB\rmF_\rmBFB​ relative to FA\rmF_\rmAFA​
  • ω⇀S/B\vect{\omega}_{\rmS/\rmB}ω⇀S/B​: angular velocity of FS\rmF_\rmSFS​ relative ot FB\rmF_\rmBFB​
  • J→B\tarrow{J}_{\mathcal{B}}J→B​: inertia tensor of the combined main body and reaction wheel(s)
  • J→B\tarrow{J}_BJ→B​: inertia tensor of the main body
  • J→W\tarrow{J}_WJ→W​: inertia tensor of the reaction wheel
  • H⇀B/c/A\vect{H}_{\SB/c/\rmA}H⇀B/c/A​: angular momentum of the body, B\mathcal{B}B, relative to the center of mass, ccc, with respect to FA\rmF_\rmAFA​
  • M⇀B/c\vect{M}_{\SB/c}M⇀B/c​: net moment applied to B\mathcal{B}B relative to ccc
  • A∙\framedota{}A∙: derivative with respect to FA\rmF_\rmAFA​
  • B∙\framedotb{}B∙: derivative with respect to FB\rmF_\rmBFB​
  • OB/S\SO_{\rmB/\rmS}OB/S​: orientation matrix that rotates TODO
  • Ω\OmegaΩ: angular velocity of the reaction wheel
  • (⋅)∣A(\cdot)\big|_{\rmA}(⋅)​A​: resolved in FA\rmF_\rmAFA​
  • (⋅)∣B(\cdot)\big|_{\rmB}(⋅)​B​: resolved in FB\rmF_\rmBFB​

Dynamics derivation

We will assume that the main body of our spacecraft can be modeled as a rigid body because the flex modes of a metal box are usually much faster than the rotational dynamics. However, this assumption might not hold if our spacecraft includes large, lightly damped solar panels and is subjected to fast maneuvers.

First, let’s start with a rigid body and single added reaction wheel. We define the following frames for our system:

  • FA\rmF_\rmAFA​ is an inertial frame
  • FB\rmF_\rmBFB​ is a body fixed frame
  • FS\rmF_\rmSFS​ is a reaction wheel frame
    • This frame is attached to the wheel and rotates about the ı^S\ihat_\rmS^S​ axis

The inertia tensors of the rigid body and wheel are J→B\tarrow{J}_{B}J→B​ and J→W\tarrow{J}_{W}J→W​ respectively. Let J→B\tarrow{J}_{\SB}J→B​ be the inertia tensor of the combined bodies about the combined center of mass.

These let us define the total angular momentum of our system

H⇀B/c/A=J→B/cω⇀B/A+J→W/cWω⇀S/B\vect{H}_{\SB/c/\rmA} = \tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} + \tarrow{J}_{W/c_W} \vect{\omega}_{\rmS/\rmB}H⇀B/c/A​=J→B/c​ω⇀B/A​+J→W/cW​​ω⇀S/B​

Taking the derivative with respect to an inertial frame, FA\rmF_\rmAFA​, we get

H⇀A∙B/c/A=J→B/cω⇀B/A⏞A∙+J→W/cWω⇀S/B⏞A∙\framedota{\vect{H}}_{\SB/c/\rmA} = \framedota{\overbrace{\tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA}}} + \framedota{\overbrace{\tarrow{J}_{W/c_W} \vect{\omega}_{\rmS/\rmB}}} H⇀A∙B/c/A​=J→B/c​ω⇀B/A​​A∙​+J→W/cW​​ω⇀S/B​​A∙​

however we don’t want to deal with the time dependent inertia tensors, we can use the transport theorem convert these into derivatives with respect to FB\rmF_\rmBFB​

H⇀A∙B/c/A=J→B/cω⇀B/A⏞B∙+ω⇀B/A×(J→B/cω⇀B/A)+J→W/cWω⇀S/B⏞B∙+ω⇀B/A×(J→W/cWω⇀S/B)=J→B/cω⇀B∙B/A+ω⇀B/A×J→B/cω⇀B/A+J→W/cWω⇀B∙S/B+ω⇀B/A×J→W/cWω⇀S/B\begin{align*} \framedota{\vect{H}}_{\SB/c/\rmA} &= \framedotb{\overbrace{\tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA}}} + \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} \times (\tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA}) + \framedotb{\overbrace{\tarrow{J}_{W/c_W} \vect{\omega}_{\rmS/\rmB}}} + \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} \times (\tarrow{J}_{W/c_W} \vect{\omega}_{\rmS/\rmB}) \\ &= \tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \framedotb{ \vect{\omega}}_{\rmB/\rmA} + \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} \times \tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} + \tarrow{J}_{W/c_W} \framedotb{ \vect{\omega}}_{\rmS/\rmB} + \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} \times \tarrow{J}_{W/c_W} \vect{\omega}_{\rmS/\rmB} \end{align*}H⇀A∙B/c/A​​=J→B/c​ω⇀B/A​​B∙​+ω⇀B/A​×(J→B/c​ω⇀B/A​)+J→W/cW​​ω⇀S/B​​B∙​+ω⇀B/A​×(J→W/cW​​ω⇀S/B​)=J→B/c​ω⇀B∙B/A​+ω⇀B/A​×J→B/c​ω⇀B/A​+J→W/cW​​ω⇀B∙S/B​+ω⇀B/A​×J→W/cW​​ω⇀S/B​​

From Newton’s 2nd law for rotations, we know

H⇀A∙B/c/A=M⇀B/c.\framedota{\vect{H}}_{\SB/c/\rmA} = \vect{M}_{\SB/c}.H⇀A∙B/c/A​=M⇀B/c​.

Spacecraft have various external moments from gravity gradients, aerodynamics, solar radion pressure, and magnetic field interactions. These would all be included in M⇀B/c\vect{M}_{\SB/c}M⇀B/c​, or they can be ignored for a first level analysis becacuse the magnitude of these terms are tiny compared to the dynamics. Putting together Newton’s 2nd law and our momentum derivative, we get

J→B/cω⇀B∙B/A=−ω⇀B/A×J→B/cω⇀B/A−J→W/cWω⇀B∙S/B−ω⇀B/A×J→W/cWω⇀S/B+M⇀B/c\tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \framedotb{ \vect{\omega}}_{\rmB/\rmA} = - \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} \times \tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} - \tarrow{J}_{W/c_W} \framedotb{ \vect{\omega}}_{\rmS/\rmB} - \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} \times \tarrow{J}_{W/c_W} \vect{\omega}_{\rmS/\rmB} + \vect{M}_{\SB/c}J→B/c​ω⇀B∙B/A​=−ω⇀B/A​×J→B/c​ω⇀B/A​−J→W/cW​​ω⇀B∙S/B​−ω⇀B/A​×J→W/cW​​ω⇀S/B​+M⇀B/c​

Define Ω\OmegaΩ as the magnitude of the reaction wheel angular velocity

ω⇀S/B=Ωı^S⇒ω⇀B∙S/B=Ω˙ı^S\vect{\omega}_{\rmS/\rmB} = \Omega \ihat_\rmS \quad \Rightarrow \quad \framedotb{\vect{\omega}}_{\rmS/\rmB} = \dot{\Omega} \ihat_\rmSω⇀S/B​=Ω^S​⇒ω⇀B∙S/B​=Ω˙^S​

Define J→W/cW\tarrow{J}_{W/c_W}J→W/cW​​ resolved in FS\rmF_\rmSFS​ as

J→W/cW∣S=[JWs000JWt000JWt]\tarrow{J}_{W/c_W} \bigg|_\rmS = \begin{bmatrix} J_{W_s} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & J_{W_t} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & J_{W_t} \end{bmatrix}J→W/cW​​​S​=​JWs​​00​0JWt​​0​00JWt​​​​

Plugging these two definitions into our system, we get to clarify the dynamics a bit

J→B/cω⇀B∙B/A=−ω⇀B/A×J→B/cω⇀B/A−JWsΩ˙ı^S−ω⇀B/A×JWsΩı^S+M⇀B/c\tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \framedotb{ \vect{\omega}}_{\rmB/\rmA} = - \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} \times \tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} - J_{W_s} \dot{\Omega} \ihat_\rmS - \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} \times J_{W_s} \Omega \ihat_\rmS + \vect{M}_{\SB/c}J→B/c​ω⇀B∙B/A​=−ω⇀B/A​×J→B/c​ω⇀B/A​−JWs​​Ω˙^S​−ω⇀B/A​×JWs​​Ω^S​+M⇀B/c​

Fundamentally, what is happening is we are trading momentum between the rigid body and the spinning reaction wheel. When we torque the reaction wheel, the rigid body torques in an equal and opposite direction. By torquing the wheel, we can control the reaction wheel acceleration, Ω˙\dot{\Omega}Ω˙, thus we have some amount of control authority in the ı^S\ihat_\rmS^S​ axis. The larger the spin axis moment of inertia and the faster we can accelerate the wheel, the more control authority we have.

Resolving in a frame

We did this derivation because want to simulate our system, but to do this we need to resolve our dynamics in a frame. This means every vector and tensor needs to be expressed in terms of the basis vectors of a frame of our choosing. Expressing our system in FB\rmF_\rmBFB​ is convenient for a couple of reasons:

  • The inertia tensors are constant in this frame
    • Even though the reaction wheel is spinning, it’s symmetrical about the spin axis so the mass distribution doesn’t change as it rotates about its spin axis
  • If we attached a gyroscope to our spacecraft, FB\rmF_\rmBFB​ is the frame we would read it in
  • Moments are usually easier to resolve in the body frame because control systems are mounted to the spacecraft
    • For example, the reaction wheel spin axis is fixed relative to the body

Define the body angular velocity as

ω⇀B/A∣B=[ω1ω2ω3]T⇒ω⇀B∙B/A∣B=[ω˙1ω˙2ω˙3]T\vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} \big|_\rmB = \begin{bmatrix} \omega_1 & \omega_2 & \omega_3 \end{bmatrix}^\rmT \quad \Rightarrow \quad \framedotb{\vect{\omega}}_{\rmB/\rmA} \big|_\rmB = \begin{bmatrix} \dot{\omega}_1 & \dot{\omega}_2 & \dot{\omega}_3 \end{bmatrix}^\rmTω⇀B/A​​B​=[ω1​​ω2​​ω3​​]T⇒ω⇀B∙B/A​​B​=[ω˙1​​ω˙2​​ω˙3​​]T

J→B/c\tarrow{J}_{\SB/c}J→B/c​ will be some 3x3 matrix of real numbers and M⇀B/c\vect{M}_{\SB/c}M⇀B/c​ will be some 3x1 vector that is dependent on your own scenario. The ı^S\ihat_\rmS^S​ terms are currently expressed in terms of FS\rmF_\rmSFS​, so we need to orient these vectors to be in FA\rmF_\rmAFA​. This can be done by representing ı^S\ihat_\rmS^S​ as a vector in FB\rmF_\rmBFB​, or using an orientation matrix to resolve ı^S\ihat_\rmS^S​ in FB\rmF_\rmBFB​ at any instant of time.

ı^S∣B=OB/S  ı^S∣S=[ı^B′ı^Sȷ^B′ı^Sk^B′ı^S]T\ihat_\rmS \big|_\rmB = \SO_{\rmB/\rmS} \; \ihat_\rmS \big|_\rmS = \begin{bmatrix} \ihat_\rmB ' \ihat_\rmS & \jhat_\rmB ' \ihat_\rmS & \khat_\rmB ' \ihat_\rmS \end{bmatrix}^\rmT^S​​B​=OB/S​^S​​S​=[^B′​^S​​^​B′​^S​​k^B′​^S​​]T

At this point, our system is fully resolved in FB\rmF_\rmBFB​ so we can simulate it!

J→B/c∣B[ω˙1ω˙2ω˙3]=−[ω1ω2ω3]×J→B/c∣B[ω1ω2ω3]−JWsΩ˙[ ı^S∣B ]−[ω1ω2ω3]×JWsΩ[ ı^S∣B ]+M⇀B/c∣B\tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \bigg|_\rmB \begin{bmatrix} \dot{\omega}_1 \\ \dot{\omega}_2 \\ \dot{\omega}_3 \end{bmatrix} = - \begin{bmatrix} \omega_1 \\ \omega_2 \\ \omega_3 \end{bmatrix} \times \tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \bigg|_\rmB \begin{bmatrix} \omega_1 \\ \omega_2 \\ \omega_3 \end{bmatrix} - J_{W_s} \dot{\Omega} \begin{bmatrix} ~ \\ \ihat_\rmS \big|_\rmB \\ ~ \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \omega_1 \\ \omega_2 \\ \omega_3 \end{bmatrix} \times J_{W_s} \Omega \begin{bmatrix} ~ \\ \ihat_\rmS \big|_\rmB \\ ~ \end{bmatrix} + \vect{M}_{\SB/c} \bigg|_\rmBJ→B/c​​B​​ω˙1​ω˙2​ω˙3​​​=−​ω1​ω2​ω3​​​×J→B/c​​B​​ω1​ω2​ω3​​​−JWs​​Ω˙​ ^S​​B​ ​​−​ω1​ω2​ω3​​​×JWs​​Ω​ ^S​​B​ ​​+M⇀B/c​​B​

Additional wheels

Moving from one reaction wheel to multiple reaction wheels is quite simple. Each wheel must be included included in the total body inertia tensor, J→B/c\tarrow{J}_{\mathcal{B}/c}J→B/c​, and we need to keep track of each wheel’s angular velocity and spin axis. Define

  • Ωi\Omega_iΩi​ as the angular velocity of the iiith wheel
  • JWs,iJ_{W_s,i}JWs​,i​ as the spin axis moment of inertia of the iiith wheel
  • ı^S,i\ihat_{S,i}^S,i​ as the spin axis of the iiith wheel

Our new dynamics with nnn reaction wheels becomes

J→B/cω⇀B∙B/A=−ω⇀B/A×J→B/cω⇀B/A−∑i=1n(JWs,iΩ˙iı^S,i)−∑i=1n(ω⇀B/A×JWs,iΩiı^S,i)+M⇀B/c\tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \framedotb{ \vect{\omega}}_{\rmB/\rmA} = - \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} \times \tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} - \sum_{i=1}^n \left( J_{W_s,i} \dot{\Omega}_i \ihat_{\rmS,i} \right) - \sum_{i=1}^n \left( \vect{\omega}_{\rmB/\rmA} \times J_{W_s,i} \Omega_i \ihat_{\rmS,i} \right) + \vect{M}_{\SB/c}J→B/c​ω⇀B∙B/A​=−ω⇀B/A​×J→B/c​ω⇀B/A​−i=1∑n​(JWs​,i​Ω˙i​^S,i​)−i=1∑n​(ω⇀B/A​×JWs​,i​Ωi​^S,i​)+M⇀B/c​

because each reaction wheel angular velocity is independent, so we just sum up each wheel’s contribution. Resolving in FB\rmF_\rmBFB​ is the same process as for the single wheel,

J→B/c∣B[ω˙1ω˙2ω˙3]=−[ω1ω2ω3]×J→B/c∣B[ω1ω2ω3]−∑i=1nJWs,iΩ˙i[ ı^S,i∣B ]−∑i=1n[ω1ω2ω3]×JWs,iΩi[ ı^S,i∣B ]+M⇀B/c∣B\begin{align*} \tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \bigg|_\rmB \begin{bmatrix} \dot{\omega}_1 \\ \dot{\omega}_2 \\ \dot{\omega}_3 \end{bmatrix} =& - \begin{bmatrix} \omega_1 \\ \omega_2 \\ \omega_3 \end{bmatrix} \times \tarrow{J}_{\SB/c} \bigg|_\rmB \begin{bmatrix} \omega_1 \\ \omega_2 \\ \omega_3 \end{bmatrix} - \sum_{i=1}^n J_{W_s,i} \dot{\Omega}_i \begin{bmatrix} ~ \\ \ihat_{\rmS,i} \big|_\rmB \\ ~ \end{bmatrix} \\ & - \sum_{i=1}^n \begin{bmatrix} \omega_1 \\ \omega_2 \\ \omega_3 \end{bmatrix} \times J_{W_s,i} \Omega_i \begin{bmatrix} ~ \\ \ihat_{\rmS,i} \big|_\rmB \\ ~ \end{bmatrix} + \vect{M}_{\SB/c} \bigg|_\rmB \end{align*}J→B/c​​B​​ω˙1​ω˙2​ω˙3​​​=​−​ω1​ω2​ω3​​​×J→B/c​​B​​ω1​ω2​ω3​​​−i=1∑n​JWs​,i​Ω˙i​​ ^S,i​​B​ ​​−i=1∑n​​ω1​ω2​ω3​​​×JWs​,i​Ωi​​ ^S,i​​B​ ​​+M⇀B/c​​B​​

As mentioned before, our control of the system is applied by torquing the reaction wheels which results in Ω˙i≠0\dot{\Omega}_i \ne 0Ω˙i​=0. This control from the iiith wheel is applied in the ı^S,i\ihat_{\rmS,i}^S,i​ axis, so to ensure we have full 3-axis controllability of the system, we want the ı^S,i\ihat_{\rmS,i}^S,i​ vectors to span 3D space.

span{ı^S,1,  ⋯  ,ı^S,n}=R3\text{span} \{ \ihat_{\rmS,1}, \; \cdots \;, \ihat_{\rmS,n} \} = \mathbb{R}^3span{^S,1​,⋯,^S,n​}=R3

or equivalently

G=[  ı^S,1∣B⋯ı^S,n∣B  ]⇒rank(G)=3G = \begin{bmatrix} ~ & & ~ \\ \ihat_{\rmS,1} \big|_B & \cdots & \ihat_{\rmS,n} \big|_B \\ ~ & & ~ \\ \end{bmatrix} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \text{rank(G)} = 3G=​ ^S,1​​B​ ​⋯​ ^S,n​​B​ ​​⇒rank(G)=3

Simulating the system

We can write up a quick simulation in MATLAB to see how the reaction wheels affect the body angular velocity. In this case, we will be constantly accelerating the wheels at 5 rad/s^2.

% Example MATLAB script simulating the dynamics of a rigid body with reaction wheels

% inertia tensors
J_b = diag([3,4,5]);
J_w = 0.01;

% spin axes
G = [1 0 0 sqrt(3)/3; 
    0 1 0 sqrt(3)/3; 
    0 0 1 sqrt(3)/3];

tspan = linspace(0, 60, 1000);

% q(1:3, i): [omega_1, omega_2, omega_3]
% q(4:7, i): [Omega_1, Omega_2, Omega_3, Omega_4]
q = zeros(7,1000);

% initial conditions
omega0 = [0.1; 0.1; 0.1];
Omega0 = [-200; -150; 100; 50]; % wheel speeds
q0 = [omega0; Omega0];

% integrate
[t, q] = ode45(@(t,q) eom(t, q, J_b, J_w, G), tspan, q0);

% Plot results
figure(1);
subplot(2,1,1);
plot(t, q(:,1:3));
title('Body Angular Velocity');
xlabel('Time (s)'); ylabel('\omega (rad/s)');
legend('\omega_1', '\omega_2', '\omega_3');

subplot(2,1,2);
plot(t, q(:,4:end));
title('Reaction Wheel Speeds');
xlabel('Time (s)'); ylabel('\Omega_i (rad/s)');
legend('\Omega_1', '\Omega_2', '\Omega_3', '\Omega_4');

function dqdt = eom(~, q, J_b, J_w, G)
    omega = q(1:3);     % body angular velocity
    Omega = q(4:end);   % wheel speeds
    n = length(Omega);
    
    % assume we are positively torquing the reaction wheels
    dOmega = 5*ones(n,1);

    Hdot_rigid_body = cross(omega, J_b*omega);
    Hdot_wheels = zeros(3,1);
    for i = 1:n
        Hdot_wheels = Hdot_wheels + J_w*dOmega(i)*G(:,i) + cross(omega, J_w*Omega(i)*G(:,i));
    end

    % assume no external moments
    M = 0;
    
    % solve for omega rate of change
    domega = J_b \ (-Hdot_rigid_body - Hdot_wheels + M);

    dqdt = [domega; dOmega];
end
